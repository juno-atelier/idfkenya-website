Our Story

Built on community.
Driven by purpose.

Integrated Development Facility (IDF) was founded in 1999 to address the challenges of HIV/AIDS, deteriorating education standards and reduced economic opportunities among the people of Homa Bay.

Today, IDF implements life-changing projects in seven counties across Kenya — working with governments, international donors, and local communities to build lasting solutions in health, governance, water, and child welfare.

We are committed to being an open, equal opportunity organization that does not discriminate on the basis of sex, race, religion, national origin or disability.

USAID Nuru Ya Mtoto Project

Governance

Kuimarisha Kaunti Project

Improving citizen access to county services and building governance capacity across all 8 sub-counties of Homa Bay. Winner of the 2023 Ndovu Award.

Health

Global Fund Malaria Project

Community case management of malaria supporting 1,140 CHVs across 114 community health units in Homa Bay County. Budget: Ksh 103,247,492.
