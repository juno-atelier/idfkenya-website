Homa Bay County
USAID Nuru Ya Mtoto Kuimarisha Kaunti Global Fund Malaria Seka Kagwa Water

IDF's home county & headquarters

IDF was founded in Homa Bay in 1999 and has maintained its headquarters here at Kadongo Centre, Rachuonyo East Sub County. It remains IDF's most operationally intensive county, hosting four concurrent projects across all eight sub-counties.

The USAID Nuru Ya Mtoto project operates in Rachuonyo North Sub County, supporting orphans and vulnerable children (OVC) and their households. The project works with the Directorate of Children Services, Ministry of Health, and County Government to deliver integrated case management and advance Kenya's 95-95-95 HIV strategy.

The Kuimarisha Kaunti governance project reaches all eight sub-counties — Rachuonyo East, Rachuonyo South, Rachuonyo North, Rangwe, Homa Bay Town, Mbita, Ndhiwa and Suba — mobilizing citizens and building county service accountability structures. This project earned IDF the 2023 Ndovu Award for outstanding governance work.

Ndovu Award 2023

Awarded once every 10 years, the Ndovu Award was presented to IDF for outstanding work in educating and mobilising citizens to participate in county governance and service delivery in Homa Bay County.

Vihiga County
USAID Nuru Ya Mtoto

Supporting children & households in Vihiga

IDF implements the USAID Nuru Ya Mtoto project across the whole of Vihiga County as a Local Implementing Partner (LIP) in cooperative agreement with PATH Kenya. The project targets orphans and vulnerable children (OVC) and adolescent girls and young women (AGYW) in the county.

Vihiga County has a COP beneficiary target of 2,346 with a focus on the four domains of the NYM programme — Healthy, Safe, Stable and Schooled. The project works directly with the County Director of Children Services and Sub-County Health Management Teams to deliver evidence-based OVC services.

IDF's entry into Vihiga was marked by a formal stakeholder engagement meeting presided over by the County Director of Children Services, ensuring community-led ownership from the outset. Field activities in Sabatia and Emuhaya sub-counties have since demonstrated strong programme uptake.

AGYW Programme Focus

Vihiga is one of the DREAMS-targeted counties under the USAID NYM project, providing a comprehensive package of HIV prevention and social support services to adolescent girls and young women.

Nyamira County
USAID Nuru Ya Mtoto

Protecting children across Nyamira County

IDF implements the USAID Nuru Ya Mtoto project across the whole of Nyamira County. The project is supporting the Government of Kenya in attaining its goal of addressing the HIV and AIDS response by safeguarding the rights and welfare of children and adolescents impacted by HIV and AIDS.

Operating under a five-year cooperative agreement with PATH Kenya (March 2021 – March 2026), IDF's Nyamira programme focuses on increasing household economic stability to care for and protect OVC, strengthening OVC-related community systems and structures, and expanding targeted services for HIV-exposed and affected children.

The Nyamira programme works with a range of stakeholders including the Directorate of Children Services, the Ministry of Health, and County Government offices, implementing interventions aligned with the United Nations' ambitious 95-95-95 strategy to end the HIV pandemic by 2030.

Education Support

Under the Schooled domain, the project supports vulnerable students to enroll, be retained and progress academically through school fee assistance, uniforms and supplies — reducing the financial burden on families affected by HIV.

Meru County
Global Fund Malaria (Completed)

Reducing malaria burden in Meru County

IDF implemented the Global Fund Malaria Project in Meru County between 2018 and 2021. The project improved access to prompt and effective treatment of malaria, contributing directly to Kenya's national goal of a "Malaria Free Kenya" as articulated in the Kenya Malaria Strategy.

The programme supported community health workers to identify suspected malaria cases in the community, test using mRDT kits, and treat confirmed cases using artemether-lumefantrine (AL). Community Health Assistants were trained to supervise commodity supply chains and compile monthly accountability reports.

Routine Malaria Quality Audits conducted across all seven project counties — including Meru — assessed medication practices and data management systems. IDF coordinated closely with the Meru County health teams to ensure national treatment guidelines were followed throughout implementation.

Project Impact

Community case management has proven effective in managing malaria at the household level. Provision of malaria commodities to CHVs for routine household visits ensured accessibility and prompt management of suspected cases across Meru County.

Tharaka Nithi County
Global Fund Malaria (Completed)

Community malaria management in Tharaka Nithi

IDF implemented the Global Fund Malaria Project in Tharaka Nithi County between 2018 and 2021 as a sub-recipient to the Principal Recipient, Amref Health Africa. The project formed part of Kenya's nationwide strategy to achieve 100% management of suspected malaria cases according to treatment guidelines.

Working with the Tharaka Nithi County Department of Health, IDF facilitated training of Community Health Extension Workers (CHEWs) and Community Health Volunteers (CHVs) on mRDT testing procedures, AL dispensing, and monthly data reporting. CHVs received biosafety kits for safe waste management of used mRDT materials.

The project's vector control component reached teachers in Tharaka Nithi through Training of Trainers (ToT) sessions focused on malaria mobilisation, with approximately 3,280 pupils involved in door-to-door health mobilisation in the county.

Quality Assurance

Routine malaria quality audits were conducted in Tharaka Nithi to assess medication practices and data management. The quality assurance framework ensured CHVs maintained proficiency in mRDT testing and treatment dispensing throughout the project period.

Kwale County
Global Fund Malaria (Completed)

Malaria control on the Kenya coast

IDF implemented the Global Fund Malaria Project in Kwale County between 2018 and 2021. Located on Kenya's coastal belt, Kwale is a malaria-endemic region where community-level case management was critical in reaching underserved households far from health facilities.

The programme trained Community Health Volunteers to conduct household visits, test suspected cases using rapid diagnostic test kits, and dispense treatment. CHVs received regular refresher training on CCMm procedures, alongside biosafety protocols for proper disposal of used test materials.

IDF collaborated closely with Kwale County health management teams and Sub-County Health Reporting and Information Officers (SCHRIOs) to upload community health unit data into the Kenya Health Information System (KHIS), ensuring real-time monitoring of malaria indicators.

Data Systems Support

The project facilitated provision of data bundles to SCHRIOs and airtime to CHEWs to support consistent uploading of CHU data into KHIS, improving national malaria surveillance coverage in Kwale County.

Kirinyaga County
Global Fund Malaria (Completed)

Building community health capacity in Kirinyaga

IDF implemented the Global Fund Malaria Project in Kirinyaga County between 2018 and 2021. Kirinyaga, located in Kenya's central region near Mount Kenya, was one of seven counties included in the programme's nationwide scope under the Kenya Malaria Strategy framework.

The programme built lasting capacity within Kirinyaga's Community Health Units by training frontline CHVs and CHEWs on community case management. Monthly data review meetings were held at CHU level to track progress and address implementation challenges in a structured, evidence-based way.

IDF's collaboration with the Kirinyaga County Department of Health ensured that all trained personnel were fully integrated into the County Health Management Team's supervision structures, creating sustainable systems beyond the project period and supporting the county's ongoing malaria control efforts.

Lasting Partnerships

The Global Fund project in Kirinyaga strengthened IDF's relationship with the county government health systems — creating a foundation for future collaboration on community health and disease prevention programmes in the region.
