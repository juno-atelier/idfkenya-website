Our History

Over 25 years of community service

1999 Founded

Integrated Development Facility (IDF) was founded in the year 1999 and was formally registered as a non-governmental organization (NGO) the same year.

The organization was founded to address the challenges of the HIV/AIDS pandemic, deteriorating education standards, and reduced economic opportunities among the people of Homa Bay.

The organization has grown and expanded over the years and currently implements projects in seven counties across the country. IDF has created and managed incredible partnerships that have transformed the lives of vulnerable communities across the country.

Our Head Office is located at Kadongo Centre, Rachuonyo East Sub County, Homa Bay County, along Kisii-Kisumu Road. We also operate satellite offices in all the project areas.

1999

Foundation & Registration

IDF founded and formally registered as an NGO to address HIV/AIDS, education and economic challenges in Homa Bay.

2018 – 2021

Global Fund Malaria — Phase 1

Implemented Global Fund Malaria Project in Meru, Tharaka Nithi, Kwale and Kirinyaga counties.

2021 – Present

USAID Nuru Ya Mtoto & Malaria Phase 2

Expanded to implement USAID NYM across Homa Bay, Nyamira and Vihiga counties alongside the Global Fund Malaria project.

2023

Ndovu Award — Governance Excellence

Awarded the prestigious Ndovu Award for outstanding work in educating and mobilizing citizens in Homa Bay County governance.

Mission & Vision

Our purpose, clearly stated

IDF's mission and vision define every project we implement and every community we serve across Kenya.

Our Vision

We envision a healthy, just, and empowered society where every community member can thrive and reach their full potential.

Our Mission

To harmonise and consolidate local efforts through community empowerment in areas of health education, socio-economic and environmental concerns.

Equal Opportunity Commitment

We are committed to being an open, equal opportunity organization that does not discriminate on the basis of sex, race, religion, national origin, or disability. This applies to all organizational practices including provision of services to clients and beneficiaries.

Core Values

The principles that guide us

Five core values shape every decision IDF makes — from the field to the boardroom.

Professionalism

We uphold the highest standards of conduct, competence and integrity in all our work and interactions with partners and communities.

Accountability

We take responsibility for our actions and decisions, ensuring transparency in the use of resources entrusted to us by donors and communities.

Stewardship

We manage all resources — financial, human and environmental — with care and wisdom, ensuring long-term sustainability for the communities we serve.

Teamwork

We believe in the power of collaboration — working together internally and with partners to achieve more than any one organization can alone.

Transparency

We are open and honest in all our communications, operations and reporting — building trust with communities, donors and government partners alike.

Christian Values

Our work is guided by Christian principles of compassion, dignity, justice and service to the most vulnerable members of society.
